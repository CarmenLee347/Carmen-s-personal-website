About

Shangge Li is a dynamic researcher and aspiring cultural leader deeply committed to advancing contemporary art through an interdisciplinary lens that bridges art, cultural studies and law. Currently an undergraduate student at Peking University, Li is pursuing a dual major in Law and History of Art. Her academic journey includes an exchange program at the University of Edinburgh in 2023 and the Summer Research Programme at the University of Hong Kong in 2024.

With her rich cross-cultural experiences and interdisciplinary foundation, Li has held research assistant roles at institutions including Peking University and the Central Academy of Fine Arts, contributing to projects that deepen her understanding of contemporary art’s theory, history, and practice, alongside its intersections with intellectual property and artists’ rights. She aspires to foster inclusive artistic communities through innovative curatorial work and engaging cultural practices.

In addition to her academic pursuits, Li is a painter, pianist, Guqin player, dancer, photographer as well as an emerging curator. She embraces her identity as a growing artist and believes in the transformative power of art.
